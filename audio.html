About

I began my journey into the world of audio early on in high school. I had joined my first band shortly after starting to teach myself guitar and the task of figuring out how to record our first songs fell to me. From that first attempt at recording, my audio obsession only grew, diving deeper each year into the career field, recording more of my own bands, friends bands, and eventually going to school for it and landing my first job in an actual recording studio in Los Angeles. I've worked in a number of studios around the country as well as breaking into corporate Audio Visual work, primarily at The Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in Cambridge, MA. I've toured the USA countless times as a musician and crew member (Tour Manager, Merchandise Manager) and have been lucky enough to play music in 27 countries thus far with various bands over the years. It's been a blast thus far and I'm continually looking forward to where it may take me next!

Hey there! My name is Robert Cheeseman and I'm an Audio Engineer turned Software Developer. I'm really drawn to Front End development as I thoroughly enjoy the creative aspect of watching a beautiful application/website come together from its inception all the way to finished product. When I'm not working on coding or audio you can find me enjoying all sorts of outdoor activities that Colorado has to offer (Camping, hiking, snowboarding, rafting...) with my wife and our pup! I've been playing in bands for over 20 years and have toured professionally both on the musician and crew side and have thus far been able to play music in 27 countries. I have an extensive record collection (yes, vinyl!), enjoy traveling, and typically have a guitar or bass in my hands at least once a day.
